Glossary

Admiral: The chief or head officer of a number of ships.

Anchor: A heavy piece of metal on a rope which is dropped form a ship to keep the ship in one place.

Bargain (v): To agree to do something, is somebody does something for you.

Cabin-boy: A boy who works for the officers in ship.

Cheese: A kind of food made from milk.

Compass: An instrument which shows where north, south, east, and west are.

Crew: All the seaman working in a ship.

Deck: The floor of a ship.

Defend: To fight in order to guard something.

Devil: The enemy of God, or a bad person.

Goat: An animal that gives milk and often lives wild.

Monkey: A wild animal that can climb trees fast and easy.

Mutiny: When sailor fight the captain and officers and take control of a ship.

Pirate: A sailor who fights and steals form other ships at sea.

Rescue (v): To save someone from danger or death.

Rope: Very thick, string string.

Rum: A strong drink liked by sailors.

Scoundrel: A bad person.

Shore: The ground along the edge of the sea.

Skeleton: The bone frame in the body of an animal or person.

Squire: An old word for the chief landowner in an area.

Swear: (past tense of swore) To use bad words.

Treasure: Gold, silver, money, or other valuable things.
